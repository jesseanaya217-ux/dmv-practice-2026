How the California knowledge test works

The real California DMV knowledge test has 36 questions for adults (18 and over), and you must answer 30 correctly — about 83% — to pass. Applicants under 18 take a longer 46-question test and need 38 correct. You get three attempts per application. The test covers road signs, traffic signals, right-of-way, speed limits, parking, sharing the road, alcohol and drug laws, and safe-driving practices.

California DMV quick facts to memorize

Speed limit — business or residential district
25 mph unless posted otherwise.
Speed limit — blind intersection or alley
15 mph.
Speed limit — within 100 ft of a railroad crossing without a clear view
15 mph.
Blood alcohol limit (BAC)
0.08% at 21 and over, 0.01% under 21, 0.04% commercial.
Following distance
At least 3 seconds behind the car ahead — more in rain or fog.
Turn signal
Signal the last 100 feet before turning, and 5 seconds before a freeway lane change.
Headlights
On from 30 minutes after sunset to 30 minutes before sunrise, and whenever your wipers are on.
High beams
Dim within 500 feet of an oncoming vehicle and 300 feet when following one.
Passing a bicyclist
Give at least 3 feet of clearance.
Parallel parking
Park within 18 inches of the curb.
Curb colors
Red = no stopping; White = quick passenger or mail stop; Green = limited time; Yellow = loading; Blue = disabled parking.
Daylighting law
No parking within 20 feet of a crosswalk.

California road signs: shapes and colors

You can identify many road signs by their shape and color alone:

  • Octagon, red — STOP.
  • Triangle pointing down, red and white — YIELD.
  • Pentagon, yellow-green — school zone or school crossing.
  • Round, yellow — railroad crossing ahead.
  • Diamond, yellow — warns of road conditions ahead such as curves, merges, or pedestrians.
  • Diamond, orange — construction or work zone.
  • Rectangle, white — regulatory laws you must obey, such as speed limits and one-way streets.
  • Rectangle, green, blue, or brown — guide signs for directions, services, and recreation areas.
